Supporting People Before They Burn Out

Burnout isn't a personal failure.

It’s what happens when people keep showing up, even when the system no longer supports them.

It’s what happens when people keep giving their best in environments that notice mistakes faster than humanity.

It's what happens when people become invisible inside systems and protocols.

My work helps organisations slow down enough to notice the individuals within them, not as problems to manage, but as people to understand.

I advocate for humane, proportionate responses that protect wellbeing, dignity, and sustainability at work.

The Three Pathways

Support for Schools

Education-based Support

Practical, trauma-informed support that helps educators work well, stay healthy, and remain in the profession. This pathway improves retention without adding pressure or extra workload.

Educator Support includes:

  • Evidence-informed workshops
  • Term-based burnout prevention support
  • Tools educators can apply immediately
  • Support that respects safeguarding and professional boundaries
EXPLORE SCHOOL-BASED SUPPORT

Support for Teams

Non-Education based Support

Structured, remote support for teams experiencing ongoing strain, focused on creating sustainable working conditions for everyone.

Organisation Support includes:

  • Burnout prevention sprints (30–90 days)
  • Manager toolkits and reflection frameworks
  • Evidence-informed workshops and check-ins
  • Clear outcomes and practical next steps
VIEW ORGANISATIONAL SUPPORT OPTIONS

Support for Individuals

Support without therapy

This pathway is for individuals who are holding it together on the outside, but need practical guidance to sustain their health and wellbeing over time.

Individual Support includes:

  • Structured 1:1 sessions
  • 6-week guided wellbeing programme
  • Stress awareness and regulation strategies
  • Practical tools for sustainable self-care
  • Certificate of completion
INDIVIDUAL SUPPORT

Speaking Engagements

Preventive Wellbeing Consultant. All work is delivered by Marsha Kerr Talley, Preventive Health & Wellbeing Consultant, with over 20 years’ experience in education and lived experience of burnout and recovery.

I am a passionate and engaging speaker who brings energy, movement, and presence into the room. My talks are grounded and relatable, shaped by lived experience and supported by relevant, up-to-date insight.

I do not speak at audiences. I speak with them. I invite participation, reflection, and moments of connection that allow people to recognise themselves in the conversation.

I often speak alongside carefully curated visuals, music, and colour, creating an atmosphere that feels alive and immersive rather than instructional. When I speak about something I care deeply about, that energy carries. It brings clarity, warmth, and momentum to the room.
